Chocolate croquant caramelia



TimeNew

Time to cook this dessert is around 40 minutes

Dishes needed New
  1. Tart rings
  2. Parchment paper
  3. Brush
  4. Pot
  5. Rubber spatula
Yields

It can be served to four people



Ingredients

For the caramelized apple:
  • 3 apples
  • 150 g caster suagr
  • 5 ml frying oil
  • 75 ml dark rum
For the pastry:
  • 1 pastry sheet
  • 50 g icing sugar
For the ice cream:
  • 260 ml semi-skimmed milk
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 30 g corn starch
  • 100 g caster sugar
  • 100 ml double cream
  • 1 vanilla pond


Preperation

For the walnut sable:

Mix the dry ingredients together, followed by the butter. Mix gently until the dough comes together. Refrigerate for one hour to firm up.Roll out on a floured surface to 0.3mm and cut out rounds 3.5″ in diameter using tart rings. Place the tart rings with the cookies on a tray lined with parchment paper and bake at 350° for 12-15 minutes or until golden brown. Brush the cookies with melted chocolate.

For the walnut croquant:

Melt gently in a pot over medium heat. Prepare tray lined with parchement paper. Pour into the same size rings as above and more the croquet around to fill the ring. Bake at 350° for 10-15 minutes or until golden brown. Cool.

Banana and ginger compote:

In a shallow pot, caramelize the sugar to golden brown. Throw in the bananas along with the ginger and move it around quickly to caramelize over medium high heat. Using a rubber spatula, mash the bananas a little. Cool.

Chocolate banana ganache:

Boil the cream. Pour over the chocolate and stir gently to emulsify completely. Mix in the butter, followed by the liquified banana puree. To make a better tasting ganache it is better to strain the whole mixture to ensure uniform emulsion, or blend it with an immersion blender. Refrigerate for 1-2 hours.

Caramel hay:

Caramelize sugar to golden brown over high heat using a stainless steel pot. Cool the caramel by submerging the pot in cold water. Once it resembles slowly pouring honey, use a fork to drizzle the caramel over parchment paper. While still pliable, gather the threads into small hay stacks.
